Project Sunflower

Did you know that fixed solar panels waste up to 30% of potential energy? Project Sunflower is a smart energy optimization system that increases efficiency using a real-time tracking system. Our model uses light sensors to optimally position the panel to receive the most light possible, which aims to maximize energy generation.

Benefits of Solar Panels

  • Cost: The average U.S. household consumes about 877 kWh per month, and a 6 kW solar system can generate around 720–900 kWh monthly, potentially offsetting most of the cost. You can also use net metering to sell excess energy back to the grid, further lowering costs.
  • Environmental Impact: A typical residential solar system (6–8 kW) reduces carbon emissions by about 3–4 tons per year, equivalent to planting over 100 trees annually. Solar power produces 20 times less CO₂ per kWh compared to coal energy.
  • Reliability: Solar reduces reliance on fossil fuels and foreign energy sources. In regions with frequent blackouts, solar panels with battery storage (e.g., Tesla Powerwall) provide backup power, protecting against grid failures.

Gimbals

Gimbals allow solar panels to track the sun's movement throughout the day, ensuring that the panels are always oriented for maximum sunlight exposure. This increases energy production by up to 25-35% compared to fixed-position panels, especially in regions with variable sunlight.
Gimbals enable the panels to adjust to weather conditions, such as high winds or heavy snow, by tilting the panels to reduce pressure or accumulation. This helps extend the lifespan of the solar panels, as they experience less stress from harsh environmental conditions.
The ability of solar gimbals to adjust the panel's orientation allows them to perform well in varying geographic locations and under different environmental conditions, ensuring optimal energy output no matter where the system is placed.
